References and Further Reading

Key References

Foundational Texts

  1. Caddy, J.F. and Mahon, R. (1995). Reference points for fisheries management. ICES Journal of Marine Science, 52(3-4), 577-580.

  2. Hilborn, R. and Walters, C.J. (1992). Quantitative Fisheries Stock Assessment: Choice, Dynamics and Uncertainty. Chapman and Hall, New York.

  3. Quinn II, T.J. and Deriso, R.B. (1999). Quantitative Fish Dynamics. Oxford University Press, New York.

Data Collection Methods

  1. Sparre, P. and Venema, S.C. (1998). Introduction to tropical fish stock assessment. Part 1 - Manual. FAO Fisheries Technical Paper No. 306/1 Rev. 2.

  2. FAO. (2020). A handbook for the economic analysis of fishery management measures. FAO Fisheries and Aquaculture Circular. Rome.

Stock Assessment Methods

  1. Haddon, M. (2011). Modelling and quantitative methods in fisheries. Chapman and Hall/CRC, Boca Raton, FL.

  2. Melnychuk, M.C., Peterson, E., Christensen, V., and Branch, T.A. (2012). Fisheries management impacts on target species status. Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, 109(1), 97-102.

Data Quality and Validation

  1. Cochran, W.G. (1977). Sampling Techniques. John Wiley & Sons, New York.

  2. Kell, L.T., Minto, C., and Gerritsen, H.D. (2016). Evaluation of the RBF4 CPUE standardization approach. Fisheries Research, 183, 220-228.

Suggested Reading Order

For Beginners: 1. Start with Lecture 1: Introduction to Stock Assessment Data 2. Read Sparre & Venema (1998) - Chapters 1-3 3. Complete Lecture 2: Advanced Data Topics 4. Explore FishBase for species examples

For Advanced Study: 1. Hilborn & Walters (1992) - Chapters 1-4 2. Haddon (2011) - Part I and II 3. Recent journal articles on specific assessment methods 4. FLR documentation for software applications

Citation Format

If using materials from this course in academic work:

Course Materials: Peter, N. (2025). Fish Stock Assessment Course (AQ 232) - Lecture Notes. University of [Institution].

Specific Lecture: Peter, N. (2025). Fish Stock Assessment Data. In Fish Stock Assessment Course (AQ 232). Lecture 1. [Date Accessed].
