Glossary

Stock Assessment Terms

A

Abundance Index A relative measure of population size derived from survey data or fishery catch-per-unit-effort. Does not provide absolute population size but indicates trends over time.

Age Determination The process of estimating fish age through methods such as otolith reading, scale reading, or vertebral section examination.

Age-Structured Model A stock assessment model that divides the population into age classes and projects each class forward through time, accounting for growth, mortality, and reproduction.

B

Bycatch Fish species or individuals caught incidentally while targeting other species. Often discarded or of lower commercial value.

Biomass Total weight of all fish in a population or stock at a given time.

C

Catchability (q) The proportion of the population in an area that is caught per unit of fishing effort. Ranges from 0 to 1.

Catch-per-Unit-Effort (CPUE) Total catch divided by total fishing effort. Used as an index of fish abundance (CPUE = q × Abundance).

Catch Assessment Survey (CAS) A systematic survey designed to estimate total catch from a fishery based on sampling landing sites or fishing effort and using raising factors.

Coefficient of Variation (CV) Standard deviation expressed as a percentage of the mean. Used to measure relative variability in estimates.

D

Discard Fish that are caught but returned to the sea, either dead or alive. Often occurs when catch regulations are exceeded or fish have low market value.

Discardability The proportion of catch that is discarded.

E

Effort A measure of fishing intensity, including number of vessels, fishing time, gear quantity, or other measures of fishing activity.

Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ) A zone extending 200 nautical miles from a country’s coastline where the country has exclusive rights to exploit natural resources.

F

Fishery-Dependent Data Data collected directly from the fishing process itself, including landing records, onboard observer data, and fisher reports.

Fishery-Independent Data Data collected through research activities independent of the fishing process, such as research surveys and tagging experiments.

Fecundity The number of eggs produced by a female fish, often related to body size.

Frame Survey A census-based survey that collects data on all fishing vessels, gear, and landing sites to establish a sampling frame and generate raising factors.

G

Gear Selectivity The property of fishing gear to catch fish of certain sizes more readily than others. Typically expressed as a selectivity curve.

Gonad The reproductive organ in fish. Examination of gonad development determines maturity status.

H

Harvest Rate The proportion of the population removed by fishing in a given time period (usually annually).

L

Landing Records Official records of fish catch that are landed and sold at ports. The most common source of fishery-dependent data.

Length-Weight Relationship A mathematical relationship between fish length and weight, typically expressed as W = aL^b.

M

Maturity Staging Classification of fish gonad development (immature, maturing, mature, spent) to determine reproductive status.

Mortality The rate at which fish are lost from a population. Includes natural mortality, fishing mortality, and other causes.

O

Onboard Observer A government personnel who rides aboard fishing vessels to collect catch, bycatch, discard, and other data.

Otolith The ear bone of fish used for age determination. Growth rings (annuli) indicate age similar to tree rings.

P

Portside Sampling Collection of biological data (length, age, sex, maturity) from a sample of catch at landing sites.

Population Parameter A characteristic of the population such as abundance, growth rate, mortality rate, or reproduction rate.

R

Raising Factor A multiplier used to expand sample data to population estimates. Calculated as the ratio of population size to sample size.

Recruitment The addition of new, juvenile fish to the exploitable population through reproduction and growth.

Reference Point A target or limit value for a population parameter (e.g., biomass, harvest rate) used in fisheries management.

S

Sampling Frame A complete listing of all units (e.g., vessels, landing sites) in a population from which a sample is drawn.

Selectivity Curve A mathematical function describing how gear selectivity varies with fish size.

Sexual Maturity The developmental stage when fish become capable of reproduction.

Standardization A statistical process to adjust catch-per-unit-effort data for effects of non-biological factors (vessel characteristics, season, environmental conditions) to obtain a more accurate abundance index.

Stock A population of fish of the same species in a geographic area that shares similar ecological characteristics and is managed as a unit.

Stock Assessment An evaluation of the current status and trends of a fish stock using collected data and mathematical models.

Surplus Production The amount of biomass growth available for sustainable harvest from a population.

V

Vessel Monitoring System (VMS) Electronic device that automatically records and transmits the location of a fishing vessel, used for monitoring fishing effort and spatial distribution.

Von Bertalanffy Growth Equation The standard mathematical model for describing fish growth: L(t) = L∞(1 - e^(-K(t-t0)))

Y

Year Class All fish born during the same breeding season or calendar year.
