AQ 232 – Fish Population Dynamics and Stock Assessment

Author

Nyamisi Peter

Published

December 28, 2025

Welcome to AQ 232

This course covers the fundamental concepts and methods used in fish stock assessments, including population dynamics, data collection, and analytical approaches for sustainable fisheries management.

Course Modules

Module 1: Course Introduction

  • Introduction to population dynamics
  • Introduction to stock assessment
  • Aim of stock assessment
  • Definition of key terms

Module 2: Data used in Stock Assessment

  • Types of data used in stock assessment
  • Fishery-Dependent Data
  • Fishery-Independent Data
  • Data Requirements for Stock Assessments
  • Data Quality and Uncertainty

Module 3: Population Dynamics

  • Basic concepts of fish population dynamics
  • Population structure
    • Age structure
    • Size structure
    • Sex ratio
  • Vital rates
    • Birth (Recruitment)
    • Death (Mortality)
    • Growth
  • Length-based assessment methods
  • Population models
  • Surplus production models
  • Age-structured population models
  • Equilibrium versus dynamic concepts

Module 4: The Unit Stock

  • Distribution and Abundance
    • Geographic distribution of fish populations
    • Spatial and temporal variation in abundance
    • Habitat requirements and factors affecting distribution
    • Environmental influences on stock distribution
  • Methods of Estimating Fish Stock Abundance
    • Direct methods (surveys and sampling)
    • Catch per unit effort (CPUE) indices
    • Mark-recapture and tagging studies
    • Acoustic surveys
    • Visual surveys and underwater observation
    • Population estimation models
    • Biomass indices and standardized indices
  • Lecture 2: Advanced Data Topics - Data quality, biological data collection, data management, effort standardization, gear selectivity, sampling design, and environmental data.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

  • Understand the principles of fish population dynamics
  • Identify appropriate data collection methods for stock assessments
  • Apply statistical methods to analyze fishery data
  • Evaluate stock status and provide management recommendations
  • Consider ecosystem and social dimensions of fisheries management
